Starry Night

Artist: Vincent van Gogh

Technique: Oil Painting

Style: Post-Impressionism

Description: Vincent van Gogh’s The Starry Night is one of the most iconic and emotionally powerful paintings in art history. Created in June 1889 during Van Gogh's stay at the Saint-Paul-de-Mausole asylum in Saint-Rémy-de-Provence, France, it captures a swirling night sky filled with energy, emotion, and movement, seen from the window of his room.

Mona Lisa

Artist: Leonardo da Vinci

Technique: Oil Painting

Style: Renaissance

Description: Leonardo da Vinci’s Mona Lisa is arguably the most famous and recognized painting in the world. Created between 1503 and 1506 (possibly continuing until 1517), this iconic portrait has fascinated viewers for centuries due to its subject’s mysterious expression, innovative technique, and subtle detail.

The Persistence of Memory

Artist: Salvador Dalí

Technique: Oil Painting

Style: Surrealism

Description: Salvador Dalí’s The Persistence of Memory (1931) is one of the most iconic and recognizable works of Surrealist art. Often referred to as the “melting clocks painting,” it presents a dreamlike world where time appears to have lost all meaning—challenging the viewer’s perception of reality, time, and memory.
